Understanding Inflation & Savings

Nominal Savings represents the actual amount of money you will have in the future, including interest earned.

Inflation-Adjusted Value shows the purchasing power of your savings in today's money.

Purchasing Power Loss is the difference between your nominal savings and what those savings would actually be worth in today's currency.

How it's calculated:

Future Value = Initial Savings × (1 + Interest Rate)Years + Monthly Contributions × [(1 + Monthly Rate)Months - 1] / Monthly Rate

Real Value = Future Value / (1 + Inflation Rate)Years
